Lancer Route — "The Unbroken Axis"

Core Theme: Discipline, growth through failure, protecting others through strength
Key Relationships: Atrox (ally), Kitik (ally), Bhaskar (the one to save), Stella (mystery), Kiki (distant ally)
Route Arc: From the Rupture through the Battle of Bhool Bhoolaiya and beyond

SCENE 1: The Rupture
Location: Nasir's room, Lucknow
Time: Early morning, Day 1
Mood: Quiet, tense, surreal

Narrative
The sky outside your window is the wrong color.

You wake to red light filtering through the curtains—not the red of sunrise, but something deeper. Something that should not exist in the ordinary world. The clouds roll across Lucknow like wounds opening in the sky.

You sit up. Your body feels strange. Not wrong, but... complete. As if something that was always missing has finally settled into place.

And then you feel it.

The tearing.

Your breath catches. Your hands grip the sheets. Your body is being torn open—no, not torn. Opened. Something ancient is pouring into you, filling every hollow you never knew you had, settling into your bones like a key turning in a lock.

Then: warmth. Peace. Silence.

You are not the same person who went to sleep last night.

You are Nasir. You are the Lancer. You are the Unbroken Axis.

And the Grail has chosen you.

SCENE 2: The First Day
Location: Lucknow streets / College
Time: Morning to afternoon, Day 1
Mood: Disoriented, building tension

Narrative
The city moves around you as if nothing has changed. People go to work. Students head to class. The red dawn is already becoming a memory, dismissed as strange weather, a trick of the light.

But you know better.

You walk through the streets, and you feel them. The other chosen. Their presence presses against your awareness like heat from a distant fire. One of them burns. One of them waits. One of them observes. One of them hungers. One of them has not yet arrived.

You go to college. You attend your classes. You move through the motions of an ordinary day, and no one notices that you are no longer ordinary.

But you are waiting. Preparing. When the time comes, you will not hesitate.

Choice Point 2: How do you spend your day?
A) Go to the gym. Train. Prepare.

Your body needs to understand what it has become. The gym is familiar ground. You move through your routine, feeling the Lancer's skills integrate with your own. Every motion is perfect. Every strike is certain. You are ready.

(Gain: Training Complete — Small boost to combat readiness)

B) Walk the city. Observe. Learn.

You need to understand what Lucknow has become. You walk through the streets, watching, listening. You feel the ley lines beneath the city. You sense the places where the Grail's presence is strongest. You are preparing in your own way.

(Gain: City Knowledge — Unlocks early awareness of events)

C) Return home. Wait. Rest.

You have prepared enough. Today is not the day for battle. You return to your room, close your eyes, and let the Lancer's presence settle fully into your soul. When the time comes, you will be ready.

(Gain: Centered — Reduced hesitation in first combat)

SCENE 3: The News Arrives
Location: College / City streets
Time: Late afternoon, Day 1
Mood: Tense, foreboding

Narrative
The whispers reach you before the news does.

There was a fight. Someone attacked. Someone fell.

You hear fragments—a street near the old market, a figure wreathed in fire, a man who fought back but was overwhelmed. The attacker did not kill. He simply... left.

You know who it was. You felt the fire this morning. The Burning Sun has begun to hunt.

You do not know who fell. But you know that the war has begun.

Choice Point 3: How do you respond?
A) Go to the site. See what happened.

You need to understand. You make your way to the street where the attack happened. The air still smells of smoke. The ground is scorched. But the one who was attacked is gone. You are not the only one who survived.

(Gain: Information — Learns about Kitik's survival)

B) Continue your routine. Do not react.

Reacting now would be a mistake. You go home. You eat. You rest. You prepare. When the time comes, you will not be caught off guard.

(Gain: Patience — Unlocks later options)

C) Search for the others.

You cannot wait. You move through the city, feeling for the presence of the other chosen. You find nothing. But you learn the shape of the battlefield. You know where they are. When the time comes, you will find them.

(Gain: Awareness — Knows general locations of other Servants)

SCENE 4: The Forest Road
Location: Forest road near the gym
Time: Dawn, Day 2
Mood: Quiet, charged, fateful

Narrative
You wake before dawn. Your routine calls. You dress. You walk.

The forest road is empty this early. The trees are quiet. The city behind you is still sleeping. It should be an ordinary morning.

But something is waiting for you in the shadows.

You see him before he speaks. A figure standing in the middle of the path. Tall. Still. Behind him, shapes move in the darkness—spirits, creatures, an army gathered in silence.

He watches you approach. He does not move. He does not speak.

He is testing you.

Choice Point 4: How do you approach?
A) Stop. Wait. Do not flinch.

You stop at the edge of his presence. You do not speak. You do not move. You let him see you—not as a threat, not as a target, but as something that cannot be moved.

The silence stretches. The shadows wait. And then, slowly, he nods.

(Gain: Atrox's Respect — Opens dialogue options)

B) Speak first.

"You are blocking the road."

He studies you. The shadows shift. An army waits for his command. But he does not give it.

"You are the one who does nothing."

"I prepare."

(Gain: Directness — Establishes honest dynamic with Atrox)

C) Walk past him.

You do not stop. You walk forward, and the shadows part around you. He watches you pass, and for a moment, you feel his gaze on your back.

He does not stop you. He does not attack. He simply watches.

(Gain: Atrox's Curiosity — He will seek you out later)

SCENE 5: The Bond
Location: Forest road / Training ground
Time: Dawn, Day 3-4 (after Atrox's defeat by Bhaskar)
Mood: Determined, wounded, hopeful

Narrative
Atrox finds you at dawn. He is wounded. His army is scattered. His body carries the marks of fire and fury.

You do not ask what happened. You do not need to.

He speaks first.

"I found him. I fought him. I could not stop him."

You wait.

"He is not what he was. He is not the friend I walked beside. He is something else now. Something that will burn everything if we do not stop him."

He meets your eyes.

"I need your help."

Choice Point 5: How do you respond?
A) Accept immediately.

"Yes."

There is no hesitation. You have been preparing for this moment your whole life. When the time comes, you will not miss.

(Gain: Bond with Atrox — Command Seal acquired)

B) Ask what he needs.

"What do you need from me?"

"To reach him. I cannot. But you—you are the one who does not stop. You are the one who reaches the unreachable."

You understand. You nod.

(Gain: Understanding — Unlocks deeper dialogue with Atrox)

C) Ask about Bhaskar. What was he?

"Before the fire. What was he?"

Atrox is silent for a long moment. When he speaks, his voice is quiet.

"He was my friend. He was angry. He wanted to change the world. And he was the only one who never left."

You nod. You understand more than you say.

(Gain: Insight — Understands Bhaskar's tragedy)

SCENE 6: Training with Archer
Location: Kiki's training room
Time: Days 3-6
Mood: Intense, evolving, brotherhood

Narrative
The room expands around you. Kiki's gift—a space that grows to accommodate whatever you need, that repairs whatever you break. A place to prepare for what is coming.

Kitik stands across from you. The Archer who survived Bhaskar. The one who was defeated but did not break.

Atrox watches from the edge. He has already fought both of you. Now he watches to see what you become together.

Kitik speaks first.

"I will not hold back."

You nod.

"Neither will I."

And then you fight.

Choice Point 6: Training Focus
A) Focus on teamwork. Learn to fight together.

You and Kitik move together, learning each other's rhythms, each other's tells. Atrox joins when he is needed. By the end of the session, you move as one.

(Gain: Team Synergy — Bonus when fighting with allies)

B) Focus on your own technique. Perfect the strike.

You withdraw from the others. Zuxi moves in your hands, finding the Sweet Spot again and again, searching for the path that will not close. By the end, you are closer than you have ever been.

(Gain: Zuxi Focus — Reduced cost for Noble Phantasm)

C) Observe. Learn. Understand.

You watch Kitik and Atrox fight. You learn their strengths, their weaknesses, the way they move when they are pushed. When you finally join them, you know exactly where to stand.

(Gain: Tactical Awareness — Predict enemy movements)

SCENE 7: The Revelation
Location: Kiki's training room
Time: Night, Day 4
Mood: Tense, emotional, confrontational

Narrative
Kitik's Reality Marble falls around you. The opera house. The court. The verdict that cannot be ignored.

You have seen him use it before—against Atrox, against the shadows that tested him. But this time is different. This time, the verdict is not for an enemy.

It is for a friend.

The court reveals it. Atrox's true intention. His plan. His sacrifice.

And Kitik's voice cuts through the silence like a blade.

"You plan to die."

Choice Point 7: How do you respond?
A) Stand with Kitik. Reject the plan.

"No."

Your voice is quiet. Certain. Atrox turns to you.

"You do not get to decide that alone. We fight together. Or we do not fight at all."

(Gain: Unity — Strengthens bond with both)

B) Ask why.

"Why?"

Atrox is silent for a long moment.

"Because he was my friend. Because I could not save him. Because if someone must fall—"

"It will not be you."

Kitik's voice is iron. The verdict stands.

(Gain: Understanding — Unlocks Atrox's full backstory)

C) Show them Zuxi. Let them see.

You do not speak. You raise Zuxi. And for the first time, you show them what you can do.

The Strike That Reaches the Unreachable. The technique that should not exist. The path that only you can walk.

When it is done, they understand.

You are the one who will end this. They will protect you. And when the moment comes, you will not miss.

(Gain: Trust — Allies fully committed to protecting you)

SCENE 8: The Battle Begins
Location: Bhool Bhoolaiya ruins
Time: Dawn, Day 7
Mood: Epic, final, fateful

Narrative
The labyrinth is gone. What remains is a crater of ash and ruin, the ancient structure reduced to rubble. At the center of it, at the highest point of the destruction, stands Bhaskar.

He is not the man you came to save. He is something else now. Something vast. Something that towers over the city and the land itself.

Atrox speaks first.

"Bhaskar. We are here."

The Burning Sun descends. The battle that will decide everything is about to begin.

Choice Point 8: Opening Move
A) Let Atrox lead. Follow his rhythm.

Atrox moves first. His army surges forward. You follow in his wake, watching, waiting for the moment when the path opens.

(Gain: Coordinated Assault — Atrox draws aggro)

B) Support Kitik. Protect the ranged attacker.

Kitik's arrows fly. You move to cover him, deflecting what he cannot dodge, creating space for him to fire. Together, you push forward.

(Gain: Archer Support — Kitik's damage increased)

C) Trust your discipline. Find the path.

You do not attack. You wait. You watch. The chaos of battle swirls around you, but you see through it, searching for the one moment, the one angle, the one strike that will end this.

(Gain: Pathfinding — Reduced cost for final strike)

SCENE 9: The Final Strike
Location: Bhool Bhoolaiya crater
Time: Dawn, Day 7 — Climax
Mood: Transcendent, final, salvation

Narrative
Time slows.

You see Kitik fall. You see Atrox hold the line. You see Bhaskar's fist rise one final time, ready to end everything.

And you see the path.

It is there. It has always been there. The strike that never happened. The moment you never reached. The path that was denied.

Now it is open.

Zuxi rises. The Sweet Spot aligns. The Outcome Alignment bends toward the one version of reality where this works. The Strike That Reaches the Unreachable gathers in your grip like light gathering at dawn.

Behind you, something appears. A figure wreathed in light. A Bhaskar who chose kindness. Who chose restraint. Who chose to be good.

His hand touches your shoulder. And Heaven's Mercy flows into Zuxi.

You do not hesitate. You do not flinch. You do not miss.

"Zuxi—"

Final Choice Point: The Strike
A) Strike to end. Strike to destroy.

The blade falls. The fire parts. The Berserker is split from Bhaskar's body and burns away like smoke on the wind. It is done.

(Ending: Salvation — Bhaskar is saved. The Berserker is destroyed.)

B) Strike to reach. Strike to save.

The blade pierces the fire. But you do not destroy. You reach. You find the boy beneath the rage, the friend who was lost, the soul that wanted only to correct an unjust world.

And you pull him back.

(Ending: Redemption — Bhaskar remembers. The path forward is open.)

C) Strike to end the war.

The blade falls. The fire dies. But as the Berserker fades, something else rises. The Grail's voice echoes in the silence.

"One has fallen. The vessel waits."

The war is not over. But you have taken the first step.

(Ending: Unfinished — Leads to sequel route)

SCENE 10: Aftermath
Location: Bhool Bhoolaiya ruins
Time: Dawn, Day 7 — After the battle
Mood: Quiet, hopeful, uncertain

Narrative
The dawn breaks over Lucknow. The sky is red no longer.

Bhaskar lies in Atrox's arms. His eyes are open. They are not furnaces. They are not voids. They are the eyes of a boy who was lost, who was angry, who wanted to change the world.

He is human again.

You stand at the edge of the crater. Zuxi is quiet in your grip. The light of Heaven's Mercy has faded, but something of it remains. A warmth. A memory. A path that has finally been reached.

Kitik rises from the rubble. His girlfriend's Command Seal still burns on his hand. He is broken, but alive. He looks at you, and nods.

Somewhere in the city, Kiki emerges from the ruins of the labyrinth. His system is broken. His territory is ash. But he is alive. He walks toward the light of dawn, searching for what he has lost.

And somewhere else, in a place between timelines, between possibilities, between the man who was and the man who could have been, Saber Bhaskar watches the dawn break. He smiles. He waits.

The war is not over. The Grail has not spoken. The fusion remains.

But for now, in this moment, at the dawn of a new day over Lucknow—

It is enough.
